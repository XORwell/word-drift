How to read WORD-DRIFT

This explorer maps how word meanings shift over time, and what may have caused each shift.

Connotation colours

  • Positive connotation
  • Neutral connotation
  • Negative connotation

A word's arc (for example neutral then negative) shows whether its tone improved (amelioration) or worsened (pejoration).

The five-level evidence ladder

Each causal hypothesis is rated by how strong its evidence is, weakest to strongest:

  1. Speculative — temporal coincidence only
  2. FrequencyCorrelation — usage frequency tracks the event
  3. ChangeSignalAlignment — computational change signal aligns
  4. LexicographicNote — a dictionary records the shift
  5. ScholarlyAttestation — peer-reviewed scholarship attests it
